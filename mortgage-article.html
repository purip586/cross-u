Overview of Home Loans(Mortgage) in Japan

By Puri, on 3rd Sep 2023


- Housing loans in Japan are typically used for purchasing, renovating, or refinancing a home.
- General prerequisites for loan applicants include age (20-69 years old), years of continuous employment in Japan for more than 1 years and minimum yearly income (JPY 3,000,000–5,000,000).
- The property should comply with the Building Standard Act and have land ownership.
- Loan amounts can go up to JPY 100,000,000 or JPY 500,000,000, covering 100% of the property price.
- Loan terms range from 1 to 35 years, and various interest rate options are available.
- Repayment methods include principal and equal interest, capital equal, and bonus repayment.
- Some loans require a guarantor and guarantee charge, while others may not.
- Group credit life insurance premiums are often included in loan interest.
- Handling fees vary by financial institution.


For Foreigners Applying for a Housing Loan:
- Japanese financial institutions prefer non-migratory applicants, such as those with Permanent Residency status, Japanese spouses, or a residency period eligible for Permanent Residency.
- Major banks and trust banks often require Permanent Residency status or a Japanese spouse for loan eligibility.


Financial Institutions Handling Housing Loans for Foreigners:
- Some financial institutions, including PRESTIA (SMBC Trust Bank Ltd.), Shinsei Bank, Suruga Bank, Aeon Bank offer housing loans to foreigners residing in Japan.


Investment Property Loans for Foreigners:

- Investment property loans are available for non-residents in Japan looking to purchase real estate for investment, asset management, or second homes.

1. The Tokyo Star Bank, Ltd.
- Loan products for customers of Japanese corporations established by foreign investors and Japanese corporations established by foreign nationals with resident status in Japan for the purpose of real estate investment in Japan.

2. Shinsei Investment & Finance Ltd.
- Personal loans are available only for non-residents of Japan who hold a passport issued by the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Government or Japan and currently reside in Hong Kong.

3. ORIX Bank Corporation
- ORIX Bank offers loans to foreign nationals residing in Japan with an annual income of 7 million yen or more for the previous year. Specific property conditions apply, such as structure, area, building age, and use.

4. PRESTIA (SMBC Trust Bank)
- PRESTIA provides loans to foreign nationals residing in Japan with an annual income of 7 million yen or more for the previous year. No guarantor is required. Property conditions also apply.

5. United Overseas Bank Limited
- This bank may offer options for foreign investors. You can inquire with them directly for specific details.

6. Bank of China Ltd.
- Bank of China offers services for Chinese nationals with residence status in Japan. You can contact them for further information.

7. Bank of Communications Co., Ltd.
- Bank of Communications provides loans for foreign nationals with resident status in Japan and an annual income of 4 million yen or more. You can check their website for more details.

Keep in mind that the eligibility criteria, loan terms, and conditions may vary between these institutions, so it's essential to reach out to them directly to discuss your specific situation and requirements for obtaining a real estate investment loan in Japan. Additionally, working with a local real estate agent or financial advisor who specializes in international investments can also be helpful in navigating the Japanese real estate market as a foreign investor.

Always check with the specific real estate agency for details, requirements, terms, and conditions when applying for a home mortgage loan in Japan. Additionally, having a real estate agency like Puri Cross U to assist can be valuable in navigating the process.
