**Overview of Home Loans(Mortgage) in Japan**

By Puri, on 3rd Sep 2023


- Housing loans in Japan are typically used for purchasing, renovating, or refinancing a home.
- General prerequisites for loan applicants include age (20-69 years old), years of continuous employment in Japan (2-3 years), and minimum yearly income (JPY 2,000,000–5,000,000).
- The property should comply with the Building Standard Act and have land ownership.
- Loan amounts can go up to JPY 100,000,000 or JPY 500,000,000, covering 70-80% of the purchase amount or up to 90% of the assessed home equity.
- Loan terms range from 1 to 35 years, and various interest rate options are available.
- Repayment methods include principal and equal interest, capital equal, and bonus repayment.
- Some loans require a guarantor and guarantee charge, while others may not.
- Group credit life insurance premiums are often included in loan interest.
- Handling fees vary by financial institution.


**For Foreigners Applying for a Housing Loan:**
- Japanese financial institutions prefer non-migratory applicants, such as those with Permanent Residency status, Japanese spouses, or a residency period eligible for Permanent Residency.
- Major banks and trust banks often require Permanent Residency status or a Japanese spouse for loan eligibility.


**Financial Institutions Handling Housing Loans for Foreigners:**
- Some financial institutions, including PRESTIA (SMBC Trust Bank Ltd.), Shinsei Bank, Suruga Bank, Bank of China offer housing loans to foreigners residing in Japan.


**Investment Property Loans for Foreigners:**
- Investment property loans are available for non-residents in Japan looking to purchase real estate for investment, asset management, or second homes.
- LTV (Loan to Value Ratio) for investment property loans is often lower than housing loans.


**Financial Institutions Handling Investment Property Loans for Foreigners:**
- PRESTIA (SMBC Trust Bank Ltd.) offers investment property loans without requiring Permanent Residency status.
- Shinsei Investment & Finance Limited, Bank of China, and Bank of Communications Limited also provide such loans, each with varying requirements.


**Financial Institutions Handling Loans for Non-Resident Foreigners:**
- Some financial institutions, like Shinsei Investment & Finance Limited and ORIX Asia Limited, provide mortgage loans for overseas investors living in specific regions (e.g., Hong Kong, Taiwan).

**Financial Institutions Providing Loans Regardless of Nationality:**
- United Overseas Bank Limited and Bank of China offer investment property loans to individuals of any nationality, but language skills may be required.

**Financial Institutions that Require Establishing a Corporation in Japan:**
- Non-residents in Japan may establish a corporation in Japan to qualify for a loan from institutions like Shinsei Investment & Finance Limited and Shinhan Bank Japan.

Always check with the specific real estate agency for details, requirements, terms, and conditions when applying for a home mortgage loan in Japan. Additionally, having a real estate agency like Puri Cross U to assist can be valuable in navigating the process.
